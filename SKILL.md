# Skill Synthesis & Cross-Domain Innovation

This document outlines the theoretical basis for SkillVector synthesis and the isomorphism between transformer attention and multi-strategy vector blending.

## 1. SkillVector Isomorphism
The DEGF (Dynamic Entropy Genuineness Framework) applies to any system exhibiting "focus" and "commitment":
- **Level 1 (Tokens)**: Entropy of attention weights over tokens.
- **Level 2 (Dimensions)**: Entropy of parent-attention per dimension in a SkillVector blend.
- **Level 3 (Runs)**: Entropy of G-scores over a sequence of synthesis runs.

## 2. Synthesis Methods
- **Attention-Based**: Uses parent similarity to weight contributions.
- **Hierarchical**: Multi-stage grouping and synthesis.
- **UltraV3**: Injects diversity to force "collapses" in similar-vector scenarios, ensuring the result is a genuine synthesis rather than a simple average.

## 3. Cross-Domain Innovation Engine
By applying DEGF grounding to hyperparameter tuning, we ensure that the "optimal" parameters represent a genuine structural fit to the data domain, facilitating cross-domain insights (e.g., applying DDE stability logic to financial time series).
